Project Overview

Teachers' Mentorship is a guided-learning experience designed for preservice teachers. The project leverages a scenario-based learning approach to simulate real-life interactions between a mentor and a trainee.

The goal is to help new teachers navigate the complexities of the education field, manage classroom challenges, and build confidence through mentorship simulations before stepping into a real classroom.

[Image: Persona Card - William Wallace (Senior Lecturer)]

The Challenge & Solution

The Challenge: Preservice teachers often feel overwhelmed by the demands of coursework and practicum placements. They struggle with classroom execution, adaptability, and sometimes experience imposter syndrome. Without structured guidance, these early-career challenges can lead to burnout.

The Solution: A branching scenario simulation where learners control their journey.

  • Interactive Storytelling: Learners step into the shoes of a mentee and make active decisions.
  • Safe Environment: A risk-free space to explore "what if" scenarios with a mentor figure.
  • Realistic Persona: The mentor, William Wallace (Senior Lecturer), provides guidance based on 15+ years of experience.
[Image: Articulate Storyline Interface - Conversation Simulation]

Features & Development

This project features the use of detailed storyboarding and AI-generated visuals to create immersive interactive content.

Technical Highlights:

  • Branching Scenarios: Promoting active decision-making by allowing the learner to choose different responses.
  • JavaScript Integration: Advanced interactions executed through triggers in Storyline.
  • Convergent Scenario Design: While learners make choices, the narrative guides them through a central stem to ensure key learning objectives are met.

Reflection: What I Would Add

Reflecting on this project, I would have loved to add even more complex branching paths, allowing the Preservice Teacher to face a wider variety of distinct situations with their pupils.

However, the current "convergent" model effectively demonstrates the core principles of mentorship: open dialogue, active participation, and the importance of seeking guidance to overcome self-doubt.
